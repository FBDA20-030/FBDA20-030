What to know about Berlin

History of Berlin

Berlin's history begins with its founding in the 13th century. In 1417, it was made the capital of the Margraviate of Brandenburg, then of Brandenburg-Prussia, and finally of the Kingdom of Prussia. In the 18th and 19th centuries, Prussia flourished swiftly, forming the foundation of the German Empire in 1871, which lasted until 1918, following the empire's loss in World War I.After 1900, Berlin rose to prominence as a global powerhouse in science, the humanities, music, museums, higher education, government, diplomacy, and military affairs. Manufacturing and finance were some areas where it played a part.Large portions of Berlin were destroyed by bombing, artillery, and brutal street-by-street warfare during World War II. Buildings would also be used to hide troops. The four major Allied powers were divided over Berlin. It epitomized the Cold War clash between the West and the East for nearly four decades. Berlin was reestablished as a capital and a prominent world city following Germany's reunification in 1990.

Government

Berlin has a central government and 12 district governments, with a chief burgomaster, or mayor, a 16-member government, and a city assembly, or parliament, on the central, or Land (state), level, and district mayors, district councils (governments), and district assemblies on the local level. There are several local and state courts in the city, as well as a constitutional court.

Population

The population of Berlin in 2021 is now estimated to be 3,566,791. In 1950, Berlin had a population of 3,337,621. Berlin's population has grown by 4,753, or 0.13 percent per year, since 2015. These population estimates and projections may be found in the most recent edition of the UN World Urbanization Prospects. These data depict Berlin's urban agglomeration, which includes both the city's population and adjacent suburban regions. Germany's capital and one of the country's 16 states is Berlin. It is also the largest city in Germany.

Attractions

Berlin contains three World Heritage Sites: Museum Island; the Palaces and Parks of Potsdam and Berlin; and the Berlin Modernism Housing Estates.Other landmarks include the Brandenburg Gate, the Reichstag building, Potsdamer Platz, the Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe, the Berlin Wall Memorial, the East Side Gallery, the Berlin Victory Column, Berlin Cathedral and the Berlin Television Tower, the tallest structure in Germany. Berlin has numerous museums, galleries, libraries, orchestras, and sporting events.
