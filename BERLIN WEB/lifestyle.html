Fashion in Berlin

Fashion is one of the most advanced tools for human self-expression, whether it's refined or rebellious, traditional or futuristic. Berlin Fashion Week has risen to become one of the top five fashion events in the world in recent years. And living in a vibrant metropolis like Berlin teaches you that style comes in a variety of shapes and sizes. Fashion is a language that is not only tough to master, but also impossible to keep up with, as it seems as if a new phrase is born every minute. Still, speaking the language of fashion is an adventure in and of itself, which may explain why the Berlin Fashion Week grows in popularity year after year.However, Berlin is a rather unassuming fashion capital. Unlike Paris or Milan, both fashion hubs synonymous with haute couture and luxury, Berlin’s historic lack of fashion pedigree is what makes it exceptional. Aside from its biannual Fashion Week, Berlin has in recent years positioned itself as a leading hub for the sustainable and tech fashion community.

Tourist attractions in Berlin

The Brandenburg Gate

The Brandenburg Gate is one of Berlin's most important monuments – a landmark and symbol all in one with over two hundred years of history. A former symbol of the divided city, it drew visitors who used to climb an observation platform in order to get a glimpse of the world behind the Iron Curtain, on the other side of the barren "death-strip" which separated East from West Berlin, geographically and politically..


Museum Island

Berlin's Museumsinsel (Museum Island) is a unique ensemble of five museums, including the Pergamon Museum - built on a small island in Berlin's Spree River between 1824 and 1930. A cultural and architectural monument of great significance, it was awarded UNESCO World Heritage Status in 1999. Berlin's own Acropolis of the arts is considered unique because it illustrates the evolution of modern museum design over the course of the 20th century and its collections span six thousand years of human artistic endeavour


Berlin's Cathedral Church

The beautiful dome of Berlin's Cathedral Church (Berliner Dom) is one of the city's most well-known monuments, marking the location of the remarkable basilica containing the city's most prominent Protestant church. The church inside is especially worth seeing because of its extensive artistic and ornamental elements.Despite the fact that the building is known as a cathedral, it is actually a parish church — and not just any parish. This was a pivotal period for the Hohenzollern dynasty, Prussian rulers, and later German Emperors. The church now serves the Protestant population in Berlin and the surrounding areas as the High Parish and Cathedral Church.


The Holocaust Memorial

The German parliament voted in 1999, following protracted deliberations, to build a central memorial site, the Memorial to Europe's Murdered Jews.Eisenman erected 2711 concrete slabs of various heights on a 19,000-square-meter space.The memorial lies on a slight incline, and its wave-like shape changes depending on where you stand. Its openness and abstractness allow you to approach the subject in your own unique way. The installation's massive size and lack of a central focus of remembering call into question the traditional definition of a monument. This provides a memorial, but not in the traditional sense


Charlottenburg Palace

Charlottenburg Palace is a multi-faceted setting of royal garden design that strives to inspire tourists and provide a respite from the frenetic pace of the capital city. It was founded in 1695, under the direction of Sophia Charlotte, a wise and worldly electress. Siméon Godeau, a student of André Le Nôtre, the famed court gardener of Versailles, was commissioned to design the most modern garden in the German-speaking world..


Berliner Fernsehturm

The TV tower (Fernsehturm in German) is a landmark of Berlin. It even has a special hashtag on Instagram – ‘that tower again’ – and it is not a coincidence, since the tower is visible almost from every point of the city.


Top rated hotels

The Mandala Hotel

In the midst of the city's activity, an oasis of zen-like serenity. This elegant five-star hotel on Potsdamer Platz features exclusive apartment-style accommodations, an innovative top-floor spa, and double Michelin-starred restaurants. A modern bar and impeccable service complete the picture.

Hotel am Steinplatz

This historic hotel, which was once frequented by superstars like Vladimir Nabokov and Brigitte Bardot, blends a magnificent Art Nouveau edifice with intimate, high-end service. The hotel has a quiet but convenient location, as well as a fantastic restaurant and bar, as well as a wonderful rooftop spa.

The Ritz-Carlton

This magnificent five-star hotel on Potsdamer Platz is popular with both business and pleasure travelers. It lacks for...well, pretty much nothing, with luxurious, well-equipped rooms, a superb French-themed bistro, an attractive bar, and expansive wellness

25hours Hotel Bikini Berlin

The Bikini Hotel is open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Berlin's diversity is as diverse as the city itself. It's in the City West, and it's as untamed as a jungle. In the designated Bikini-Haus building between the Berlin Zoo and Breitscheidplatz, the hotel represents cosmopolitan Berlin.
