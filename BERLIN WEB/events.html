Berlin's annual events

International Green Week-January 16th till 25th

In 1926, the International Green Week (IGW) was founded. Since then, food producers from all around the world have used the IGW to test market food, promote new goods, and build brand identities. Product presentations are frequently categorized thematically and organized by category, manufacturing method, or country of origin.

January 19th till 23st:Berlin Fashion Week

Berlin Fashion Week takes place twice a year and features Mercedes-Benz-sponsored catwalk events as well as various additional fashion fairs and tradeshows. On the runway, primarily Berlin-based designers and well-known German companies such as Marc Cain and Laurèl display their new collections, but an increasing number of international names are also included as up-and-coming talents.

January 22rd till 27th: Berliner Sechstagerennen

Several teams, each consisting of two racing bicycles, participate on six consecutive evenings. Every night, there is a fresh race with varying degrees of speed and stamina. The traditional international "pacemaker race," a derby, and a sprint race are among the events. Champion athletes from around the world compete against amateurs in all of these events. In the intervals between races, a DJ performs as part of an entertainment package.

February 5th till 15th: Berlinale

The Berlin International Film Festival (German: Internationale Filmfestspiele Berlin), sometimes known as the Berlinale, is an annual film festival in Berlin, Germany. The festival, which began in West Berlin in 1951 and has been held every February since 1978, is one of the "Big Three," together with the Venice Film Festival in Italy and the Cannes Film Festival in France, as one of the "Big Three." Mariette Rissenbeek has been the festival's executive director since 2019, and Carlo Chatrian is the creative director.

March 20th till 29th: MaerzMusik (Festival of Contemporary Music)

MaerzMusik – Festival for Time Issues sees itself as a venue for experiencing art and thinking about how we deal with time. Contemporary music is its centre and point of departure for an exploration of the nature of time in life, art and theory. MaerzMusik is organized by the Berliner Festspiele.

March 29th: Berliner Halbmarathon (Berlin half marathon)

The Berlin Half Marathon is an annual long-distance running event held in Berlin, Germany. The official name of the event is Generali Berliner Halbmarathon. About 37.000 athletes take part in half marathon race and other competitions (28.460 completed half marathon in 2019, 23 finishers were over 80 years old).

April 22nd till 26th: Sehsuechte (International student's film festival)

Sehsuechte is an international student film festival with an over 60 years lasting tradition. It takes place annually in spring in Potsdam-Babelsberg, Germany.The festival was founded by the former GDR as FDJ (Free German Youth) student filmdays based at the GDR-filmschool "Konrad Wolf".This event is growing in influence for the work of film students and up-and-coming young filmmakers. Numerous awards are handed out, and plenty of supporting events are on offer.

May 1st: Myfest (May Day street event)

May 1 is a state holiday and all businesses and shops are closed. The Berlin May Day is held in the Kreuzberg district, between Mariannenplatz, Oranienplatz, Kottbusser Tor and Görlitzer Park. You will see protests and manifestations as well as celebrations (MyFest Festival). The streets are full of people drinking beer and the famous non-alcoholic drink in Germany, Club Mate. But above all, you will see a large number of police and not without reason

June 27th: CSD - Christopher Street Day (LGBT street parade)

The Berlin Pride Celebration, usually known as CSD Berlin, is a pride parade and festival held in the second half of July each year in Berlin to celebrate the lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender people and their allies. CSD stands for Christopher Street Day. Since 1979, the event has been held each year

July 7th till 17th: Berlin Fashion Week

Berlin Fashion Week takes place twice a year and features Mercedes-Benz-sponsored catwalk events as well as various additional fashion fairs and tradeshows. On the runway, primarily Berlin-based designers and well-known German companies such as Marc Cain and Laurèl display their new collections, but an increasing number of international names are also included as up-and-coming talents

August 15th: Potsdamer Schlössernacht

The Potsdam Palace Night is an event in the park of Sanssouci Palace in Potsdam, which has been held annually since 1999. The number of visitors has been limited to 32,000 since 2002.The palaces and gardens are illuminated, around 450 artists appear all over the park. The organizer has been Kultur im Park GmbH on behalf of the Prussian Palaces and Gardens Foundation Berlin-Brandenburg since 2017

September 25th till October 18th: Berliner Oktoberfest

The Oktoberfest Berlin has been celebrated since 1949. The festival ground also provides a number of fun activities for families such as bumper cars, merry-go-rounds, rides and darts.Berliner Octoberfest in Berlin, is similar to its “big brother Octoberfest in Munich”. In Oktoberfest of Berlin the most fiestas is drinking beer. The festival that last for two weeks, takes place annually from late September till start October with the participation of more that 200,000 visitors

December 31st:New Year's Eve in Berlin at the Brandenburg Gate

The New Year's Eve celebration at the Brandenburg Gate in Berlin has its origins alongside the customs of the old year eve in the exuberant celebration at the turn of the year 1989/1990 after reunification.On New Year's Eve, the city of Berlin invites guests and Berliners alike to celebrate the beginning of a new year with a huge open air party. The celebrations with live bands, DJs and a spectacular fireworks display at midnight are free of charge and attract hundreds of thousands of people every year.
